Weather Summary
 
Kari Kiefer
Weather Underground Forecast for Thursday, May 20, 2010.

Severe weather continues sweeping through the Southern US on Thursday, triggered by a strong low pressure system. This system creates two frontal boundaries over the region. A warm front will extend eastward over the Lower Mississippi River Valley and into the Southeastern states, while a cold front follows the system tracking through the Southern Plains. Just ahead of these fronts are expected to be favorable conditions for scattered thunderstorm development, as ample moisture feeds the system from the Gulf of Mexico. This system has a history of producing severe weather with periods of heavy downpours, damaging winds, large hail, and tornadoes. If severe weather develops it will most likely be contained to the Lower and Mid-Mississippi River Valleys. This will kick up some widespread showers that will stretch over the Central and Northern Plains, into the Upper Midwest.

In the East, the low starts to shove a dominating high pressure system eastward and into the Northeast. This will allow for warm conditions to stretch from the Midwest up the Ohio River Valley, into New England and the Northeast. Expect highs to reach into the 70s in these areas.

In the West, another low pressure system that brought light rain to the Pacific Northwest and northern California will slowly move into the Northern Rockies. This will kick up light and scattered snow showers, while light rain persists in the Pacific Northwest. Expect another cool and windy day over the West Coast.

Temperatures in the Lower 48 states Wednesday ranged from a low of 26 degrees at Grand Canyon, Ariz. to a high of 96 degrees at Pecos, Texas.
